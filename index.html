Alan Turing

The man who cracked the code

Here's a time line of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1920 - Born in London, England
  • 1931 - He enteres the University of Cambridge to study mathematics.
  • 1934 - He is elected to a fellowship at King’s College after graduating from Cambridge.
  • 1936 - Finishes paper “On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem”
  • 1938 - Moves on to study for a Ph.D in Mathematical logic at Princeton University.
  • 1939 - Moves to work full-time at the Government Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park
  • 1941 - Turing's statistical approach, together with captured information, had led to the German navy signals being decoded at Bletchley.
  • 1943 - Was in the United States liaising over decoding issues and also on a speech secrecy system..
  • 1945 - Is awarded the O.B.E. in 1945 for his vital contribution to the war effort.
  • 1946 - At the end of the war Turing was invited by the National Physical Laboratory in London to design a computer. His report proposing the Automatic Computing Engine (ACE) was submitted.
  • 1950 - Published Computing machinery and intelligence in Mind.
  • 1951 - Is elected a Fellow of the Royal Society of London
  • 1952 - Is arrested for violation of British homosexuality statutes when he reported to the police details of a homosexual affair (due to threat of blackmail).
  • 1954 - Dies by suicide via potassium poisoning at the age of 42.
  • 2009 - PM Gordon Brown issues an unequivocal apology on behalf of the government to Alan Turing, the second world war codebreaker who took his own life after being sentenced to chemical castration for being gay.

“Alan Turing's pioneering work in mathematics and computer science played a crucial part in ending the Second World War. It is only fitting that we remember his legacy and the brilliant contribution LGBT people have made to our country on the new £50 note."

-- UK Prime Minister Theresa May
